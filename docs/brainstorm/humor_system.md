# Humor System

## Introduction

This system is based on the classical theory of the **four humors**, an ancient medical and philosophical framework that describes the fundamental components of living beings. Each humor is associated with specific qualities and elemental properties:

### The Four Humors

1. **Blood** (Air/Hot & Moist)
   - Represents vitality, passion, charisma, and life force
   - Associated with warmth, movement, and sociability
   - Governs speed, charm, and the capacity for connection

2. **Yellow Bile** (Fire/Hot & Dry)
   - Represents aggression, transformation, and raw energy
   - Associated with heat, combustion, and violent change
   - Governs rage, physical power, and destructive force

3. **Black Bile** (Earth/Cold & Dry)
   - Represents solidity, melancholy, and material weight
   - Associated with earth, stability, and permanence
   - Governs physical density, endurance, and grounding

4. **Phlegm** (Water/Cold & Moist)
   - Represents calmness, fluidity, and adaptability
   - Associated with water, flow, and emotional detachment
   - Governs resilience, flexibility, and connection to aquatic realms

### The Void Component

In addition to the four classical humors, this system introduces a fifth element: **the Void**. Unlike the humors, which always sum to 100% and represent a creature's physical and emotional composition, the Void operates independently as a **supernatural multiplier** or **reality anchor**.

The Void percentage determines how tethered a creature is to the physical world:

- **Low Void (0-25%)**: The creature is heavily material, bound by physical laws, dense and tangible
- **Medium Void (25-50%)**: The creature exists in both worlds, with increasing supernatural properties
- **High Void (50-75%)**: The creature is more spirit than matter, with significant ethereal abilities
- **Very High Void (75-100%)**: The creature is barely tethered to reality, nearly pure spirit

## The System

In this system, the four humors always add up to **100%** (representing the physical/emotional makeup), while the **Void %** acts as a "Supernatural Multiplier" or a "Reality Anchor."

- **Low Void (0-10%)**: The creature is hyper-physical, heavy, and bound by the laws of physics.
- **High Void (70-90%)**: The creature is "thin," phasing through walls, resistant to physical weapons, but vulnerable to spiritual attacks.

### 2. The Vampire (European)

- **Humors**: Blood 60% | Black Bile 30% | Phlegm 10% | Yellow Bile 0%
- **Void**: **40%**
- **Logic**: High **Blood** keeps them charismatic and fast, but the **Void** makes them "partially not there," allowing for mist-transformation. They drink blood to lower their Void when it gets too high, preventing them from drifting into the spirit world (and disappearing).

### 3. The Werewolf (European)

- **Humors**: Yellow Bile 70% | Blood 20% | Black Bile 10% | Phlegm 0%
- **Void**: **5%**
- **Logic**: Almost no Void. They are brutally, painfully material. The overwhelming **Yellow Bile** (Fire) causes the physical body to warp and stretch in a violent "combustion" of flesh.

### 4. The Wendigo (Algonquian)

- **Humors**: Phlegm 50% | Black Bile 50% | Blood 0% | Yellow Bile 0%
- **Void**: **85%**
- **Logic**: A terrifying mix. They have no "warm" humors. With **85% Void**, they are nearly invisible spirits of famine. They are so untethered from reality that they can run on treetops and outpace the wind.

### 5. The Golem (Jewish)

- **Humors**: Black Bile 100% | Others 0%
- **Void**: **2%**
- **Logic**: The ultimate material. At 2% Void, they are basically a sentient boulder. They are immune to almost all spiritual effects because there is simply no "space" in their soul for the spirit world to take hold.

### 6. The Kappa (Japanese)

- **Humors**: Phlegm 80% | Yellow Bile 10% | Black Bile 10% | Blood 0%
- **Void**: **15%**
- **Logic**: Heavily **Phlegmatic** (water-based). Their low Void keeps them bound to their ponds. If the water in their head-bowl (their physical anchor) spills, their Void spikes to 100%, and they "evaporate" back to the spirit realm.

### 7. Baba Yaga (Slavic)

- **Humors**: Black Bile 40% | Yellow Bile 40% | Blood 10% | Phlegm 10%
- **Void**: **60%**
- **Logic**: A high-level master. She maintains a high Void to live in her pocket-dimension (the hut), but uses **Yellow/Black Bile** to remain a terrifyingly physical threat to those who enter her woods.

### 8. The Chupacabra (Latin American)

- **Humors**: Yellow Bile 50% | Blood 30% | Black Bile 20% | Phlegm 0%
- **Void**: **10%**
- **Logic**: Like the Werewolf, it is a creature of "Heat" and "Dryness." It is a physical predator that exists purely in the material hunt.

### 9. The Banshee (Irish)

- **Humors**: Phlegm 90% | Blood 10% | Others 0%
- **Void**: **95%**
- **Logic**: The "Ascension" is almost complete. She is 95% Void, meaning she is barely a ripple in the air. She can't touch the physical world, so she uses her **Blood (Air/Voice)** humor to kill via sound.

### ðŸŽ² RPG Mechanic Idea: "The Void Threshold"

When a player's **Void** rises:

- **Void 25%**: Can see ghosts.
- **Void 50%**: Can walk through thin walls (DC check). Physical damage dealt is reduced by 25%.
- **Void 75%**: Physical weapons pass through the player, but the player can no longer pick up physical objects.
- **Void 100%**: Character is retired (Ascended or Lost).
